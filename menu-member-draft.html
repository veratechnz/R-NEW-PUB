PROPOSED CHANGES TO RESIDENTIAL TENANCIES ACT (JULY 2015)

he Cabinet Paper on insulation and smoke alarms and other residential tenancy law improvements can viewed in the panel to the right. Amendments to the Residential Tenancies Act as proposed in the Cabinet Paper will be introduced later this year.  Some of the matters covered in the Cabinet Paper are:

  • New insulation standards will require ceiling (at least 70 mm thick) and ‘reasonable’ underfloor insulation – social housing by 1 July 2016 and for the remainder, by 1 July 2019.
  • New requirement that all new tenancy agreements from 1 July 2016 declare the level of insulation in the ceilings, walls and underfloor
  • Require a minimum of 1 working smoke alarm in the hall or similar, within 3 m of each bedroom door.
  • If new smoke alarm, must be long-life photoelectric alarms. 
  • Landlord must ensure alarm is operational when tenancy begins.
  • Tenant is responsible for replacing batteries during tenancy.

DRAFT FUTURE URBAN LAND SUPPLY STRATEGY

Auckland Council have released the draft Future Urban Land Supply Strategy. The Strategy is out for public consultation from 17 July to 17 August 2015. The proposed Auckland Unitary Plan includes 11,000 hectares of Future Urban zone land. This is approximately one and a half times the size of urban Hamilton. The land is located within the proposed Rural Urban Boundary (RUB) but outside of the 2010 Metropolitan Urban Limit (MUL). The Future Urban zone land is intended for development within the next 30 years and the Strategy is to provide a logical sequence of when each area will be ready for development across Auckland. This will allow for clarity and certainty for everyone so these areas can be provided with bulk infrastructure. Due to the size and cost of this process it is impossible to invest in all areas at once and so it is imperative to have a plan for when each area will be development-ready.

BUILDING (EARTHQUAKE-PRONE BUILDINGS AMENDMENT BILL 2013 (MAY 2015)

The Government has announced a number of policy changes regarding the way earthquake prone buildings are assessed and strengthened throughout New Zealand. The country will be categorised into three zones of earthquake risk – high, medium, and low.  Each zone will be allocated a different timeframe for assessing and strengthening of buildings that may be earthquake prone. For example, high risk zones such as Christchurch and Wellington will need to be assessed within five years and strengthened within 15 years.

Once assessment is done, building owners will receive results of assessments which will also be available on a publicly accessible national register.

Select Committee is currently considering the Bill and is due to report back its findings at the end of July.

HEALTH & SAFETY REGULATIONS – REINZ’S 2ND SUBMISSION (MAY 2015)

MBIE is currently consulting on the phase one of its proposed health and safety regulations that will go with the Health & Safety Reform Bill once the Bill is passed.  This phase 1 contains draft regulations on:

  • General Risk and Workplace Management
  • Major Hazard Facilities
  • Work involving asbestos

MBIE has also issued a further set of proposed regulations on Infringement Offences and Fees Regulations setting out infringement penalty amounts.   Under these draft, the fees for infringement notices issued under the regulations range, for an individual, from $600 to $3,000 and for an entity, from $3,000 to $12,000.
