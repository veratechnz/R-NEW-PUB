The Real Estate Institute of New Zealand (REINZ)

The Real Estate Institute of New Zealand (REINZ) is a membership organisation representing more than 14,000 voluntary real estate professionals nationwide. REINZ specialises in all facets of real estate including residential sales, rural, auctioneers, business brokers, commercial and industrial sales and leasing and residential property managers.

Members of the REINZ aspire to best practice standards and are bound by the REINZ Codes of Agency Practice and Individual Membership. The REINZ brand is a mark of quality having been in business for more than 100 years. During this time the organisation has been a key part of the changing face of real estate and today provides members with current statistics, an online platform for real estate forms, advisory tips, advocacy, continuing education, national events and much more.

REINZ is the heart of New Zealand Real Estate and is constantly working on improvements in technology, presentation of data, communications and education to benefit both members and members of the public.

Codes of Practice

Although REINZ is not the industry regulator we understand it is essential for our members to adhere to the rules set out by the Real Estate Agents Authority (REAA). In addition, REINZ has their own codes of practice which members are also required to abide by as part of their membership.

Designations

MREINZ, AREINZ, FREINZ

What does it all mean? We have recognised that it may be difficult to decipher these abbreviations so we have created a document to help clarify.

MREINZ

An agency that is a current “Member” of the Real Estate Institute may advertise with the word acronym MREINZ after the company name. You may also have come across the following in relation to individual REINZ members. They are assigned by the REINZ board as acknowledgement of a particular standard of professionalism, quality and experience in this order.

AREINZ

An “Associate” member of REINZ is an individual who has achieved Agent Licensee status or be entitled to hold Agent Licensee status with the REAA, which requires “3 years experience in real estate agency work within the 10 years preceding the application” and a tertiary level diploma or equivalent in real estate – both of which display a certain level of competency in the real estate industry. The agent must have been a member of REINZ for the last four years and demonstrated active support of the Institute and its activities.

FREINZ

“Fellowship” of the Institute may be awarded to those who have been Associate members for the preceding 10 years; who conduct themselves to a high professional standard and who have provided outstanding service to the institute and real estate industry.

A Life Member

The highest honour bestowed upon members - a member who has provided an outstanding and meritorious service to REINZ and the real estate industry, and has conducted him or herself to a high professional standard.

*For full requirements of Agent Licensee status, please visit the REAA website: www.reaa.govt.nz.
